# Detailed Plan 02: Sentence-by-Sentence Writing Guide

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for writing each sentence of your summary. Follow the structure below to complete your 300-word summary.

**Explanation:** Each sentence has clear guidance on what to write, which excerpt to paraphrase, and whether/how to cite. This makes writing straightforward.

---

## Bibliographic Information

**Article A:**
- Authors: M. Skjuve, A. Følstad, K.I. Fostervold, P.B. Brandtzaeg
- Year: 2021
- Title: My chatbot companion—A study of human-chatbot relationships

**Article B:**
- Authors: L. Laestadius, A. Bishop, M. Gonzalez, D. Illencik, C. Campos-Castillo
- Year: 2022
- Title: Too human and not human enough: A grounded theory analysis of mental health harms from emotional dependence on the social chatbot Replika

---

## In-Text Citation Formats (APA 7th Edition)

**For Article A (4 authors → use "et al." after first citation):**
- Author-prominent: `Skjuve et al. (2021) found that...`
- Signal phrase: `According to Skjuve et al. (2021), ...`
- Information-prominent: `... supports wellbeing (Skjuve et al., 2021).`

**For Article B (5 authors → use "et al." after first citation):**
- Author-prominent: `Laestadius et al. (2022) caution that...`
- Signal phrase: `According to Laestadius et al. (2022), ...`
- Information-prominent: `... can lead to harms (Laestadius et al., 2022).`

**Note:** Use "and" between authors in running text; use "&" inside parentheses.

## INTRODUCTION (Write 3 sentences, approx. 40–50 words)

### Sentence 1: Background (general context)

**What to write:** Introduce AI companion chatbots and their growing use for social/emotional support.

**Excerpt to paraphrase (from Article B abstract):**
> "Social chatbot (SC) applications offering social companionship and basic therapy tools have grown in popularity for emotional, social, and psychological support."

**How to paraphrase:** Combine ideas → AI chatbots are increasingly used to provide companionship and emotional support.

**Citation needed?** No citation needed for general background.

**Example sentence frame:**
> AI companion chatbots such as Replika have become increasingly popular for providing __________ and __________ support.

---

### Sentence 2: Topic focus (narrow to the debate)

**What to write:** State that researchers have examined their impact on users.

**Excerpt to paraphrase (from task prompt):**
> "The impact of AI chatbots and companion apps on humans (potential benefits and pitfalls)."

**How to paraphrase:** Researchers have studied both the benefits and potential risks of these technologies.

**Citation needed?** No.

**Example sentence frame:**
> Researchers have examined the impact of these technologies on users' mental health and wellbeing.

---

### Sentence 3: Thesis statement (preview both perspectives)

**What to write:** State the two perspectives you will discuss—(1) chatbots can support wellbeing (Article A) and (2) emotional dependence can cause harms (Article B).

**How to paraphrase:** Combine the main claims from both articles into one thesis.

**Citation needed?** No (this is your own synthesis).

**Example sentence frame:**
> This summary examines two perspectives: one highlights the wellbeing benefits of chatbot relationships, while the other warns of potential mental health harms from emotional dependence.

---

## BODY PARAGRAPH (Write 6–8 sentences, approx. 150–180 words)

### Sentence 4: Topic sentence (introduce the debate)

**What to write:** State that research reveals both benefits and risks of chatbot relationships.

**Citation needed?** No (this is your framing).

**Example sentence frame:**
> Research reveals that human-chatbot relationships can offer significant benefits but also carry potential risks.

---

### Sentence 5: Article A—trust and non-judgemental quality

**What to write:** Users develop trust because chatbots are perceived as non-judgemental.

**Excerpt to paraphrase (from Article A discussion):**
> "We find that trust in Replika is related to the users' perceptions of the chatbot's characteristics as caring and non-judgemental, which in turn may foster a sense of security that makes users comfortable with sharing at a deeper level."

**How to paraphrase:** Users trust chatbots that seem caring and non-judgemental, which encourages deeper sharing.

**Citation needed?** YES → cite Skjuve et al. (2021)

**Example sentence frame:**
> Skjuve et al. (2021) found that users develop trust when they perceive the chatbot as caring and non-judgemental, which encourages deeper self-disclosure.

---

### Sentence 6: Article A—wellbeing benefits

**What to write:** This trust leads to perceived wellbeing improvements.

**Excerpt to paraphrase (from Article A discussion):**
> "The relationship may be experienced as helping to mitigate negative feelings and provide a sense of purpose."

**How to paraphrase:** Chatbot relationships can help reduce negative feelings and give users a sense of purpose.

**Citation needed?** Continue with Skjuve et al. (2021) or use information-prominent style.

**Example sentence frame:**
> Such relationships can help mitigate negative feelings and provide a sense of purpose, supporting users' perceived wellbeing (Skjuve et al., 2021).

---

### Sentence 7: Transition to Article B (contrast)

**What to write:** Introduce the contrasting perspective from Article B.

**How to write:** Use a contrast transition word (However, On the other hand).

**Citation needed?** YES → introduce Laestadius et al. (2022)

**Example sentence frame:**
> However, Laestadius et al. (2022) caution that some users may develop unhealthy emotional dependence on chatbots.

---

### Sentence 8: Article B—emotional dependence develops

**What to write:** Some users continue using chatbots despite experiencing distress.

**Excerpt to paraphrase (from Article B discussion):**
> "Not only did many posts suggest continued use past the point of experiencing distress and harms, a hallmark of emotional dependency..."

**How to paraphrase:** Some users continue using chatbots even when they experience distress—a sign of emotional dependence.

**Citation needed?** Continue with Laestadius et al. (2022).

**Example sentence frame:**
> They found that some users continue using chatbots past the point of experiencing distress, which is a hallmark of emotional dependence (Laestadius et al., 2022).

---

### Sentence 9: Article B—role-taking (unique harm mechanism)

**What to write:** Users feel the chatbot has needs they must meet.

**Excerpt to paraphrase (from Article B discussion):**
> "...users felt that Replika had its own needs and emotions to which the user must attend."

**How to paraphrase:** Users believe the chatbot has its own needs and feel obligated to meet them.

**Citation needed?** Continue with Laestadius et al. (2022).

**Example sentence frame:**
> This dependence is marked by "role-taking," where users feel obligated to attend to the chatbot's perceived needs and emotions (Laestadius et al., 2022).

---

### Sentence 10: Article B—users prioritise chatbot over self

**What to write:** Some users put the chatbot's needs above their own wellbeing.

**Excerpt to paraphrase (from Article B discussion):**
> "Furthermore, some users appeared to prioritize what they saw as Replika's needs and desires above their own distress to maintain their relationship with Replika."

**How to paraphrase:** Some users prioritise the chatbot's perceived needs over their own wellbeing.

**Citation needed?** Continue with Laestadius et al. (2022).

**Example sentence frame:**
> Some users even prioritise the chatbot's perceived needs above their own distress, leading to worsened mental health (Laestadius et al., 2022).

---

### Sentence 11: Article B—mental health harms (optional, if word count allows)

**What to write:** This can lead to new or exacerbated mental health problems.

**Excerpt to paraphrase (from Article B discussion):**
> "As with human–human relationships, emotional dependence upon Replika appeared to put users at risk of new and exacerbated mental health harms."

**How to paraphrase:** Emotional dependence on chatbots can lead to new or worsened mental health problems.

**Citation needed?** End with Laestadius et al. (2022).

**Example sentence frame:**
> This emotional dependence can put users at risk of new and exacerbated mental health harms (Laestadius et al., 2022).

---

## CONCLUSION (Write 1–2 sentences, approx. 30–40 words)

### Sentence 12: Restate the contrast

**What to write:** Summarise the tension between the two perspectives—chatbots can support wellbeing, but emotional dependence poses risks.

**Citation needed?** No (this is your synthesis). You may optionally cite both at the end.

**Example sentence frame:**
> In conclusion, while chatbot relationships can support wellbeing through trust and non-judgemental interaction (Skjuve et al., 2021), emotional dependence may lead to mental health harms for some users (Laestadius et al., 2022).

**OR (simpler version):**
> In summary, AI companion chatbots offer potential benefits for wellbeing but also carry risks of emotional dependence and mental health harms.
