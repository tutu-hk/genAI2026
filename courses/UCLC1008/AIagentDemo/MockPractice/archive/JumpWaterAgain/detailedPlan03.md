## In-Text Citation Formats (APA 7th Edition)

**For Article A (4 authors → use "et al."):**
- Author-prominent: `Skjuve et al. (2021)` + reporting verb
- Signal phrase: `According to Skjuve et al. (2021), ...`
- Information-prominent: `... (Skjuve et al., 2021).`

**For Article B (5 authors → use "et al."):**
- Author-prominent: `Laestadius et al. (2022)` + reporting verb
- Signal phrase: `According to Laestadius et al. (2022), ...`
- Information-prominent: `... (Laestadius et al., 2022).`

**Note:** Use "and" in running text; use "&" inside parentheses.

### Sentence 5: Article A—trust and non-judgemental quality

**Purpose:** Users develop trust because chatbots seem non-judgemental

**Excerpt to paraphrase:**
> "We find that trust in Replika is related to the users' perceptions of the chatbot's characteristics as caring and non-judgemental, which in turn may foster a sense of security that makes users comfortable with sharing at a deeper level."

**Paraphrase direction:** perceive chatbot as caring/non-judgemental → trust develops → deeper sharing

**Citation:** YES → Skjuve et al. (2021)

---

### Sentence 6: Article A—wellbeing benefits

**Purpose:** This trust leads to perceived wellbeing improvements

**Excerpt to paraphrase:**
> "The relationship may be experienced as helping to mitigate negative feelings and provide a sense of purpose."

**Paraphrase direction:** relationship helps → reduce negative feelings → sense of purpose → wellbeing

**Citation:** YES → Skjuve et al. (2021)

### Sentence 7: Transition to Article B

**Purpose:** Introduce contrasting perspective with transition

**Transition words:** However / On the other hand / In contrast

### Sentence 8: Article B—emotional dependence develops

**Purpose:** Some users continue using despite distress

**Excerpt to paraphrase:**
> "Not only did many posts suggest continued use past the point of experiencing distress and harms, a hallmark of emotional dependency..."

**Paraphrase direction:** continued use → despite distress → sign of emotional dependence

**Citation:** YES → Laestadius et al. (2022)

---

### Sentence 9: Article B—role-taking

**Purpose:** Users feel chatbot has needs they must meet

**Excerpt to paraphrase:**
> "...users felt that Replika had its own needs and emotions to which the user must attend."

**Paraphrase direction:** users believe chatbot has needs → feel obligated to meet them

**Citation:** YES → Laestadius et al. (2022)

---

### Sentence 10: Article B—users prioritise chatbot over self

**Purpose:** Some users put chatbot's needs above their own wellbeing

**Excerpt to paraphrase:**
> "Furthermore, some users appeared to prioritize what they saw as Replika's needs and desires above their own distress to maintain their relationship with Replika."

**Paraphrase direction:** prioritise chatbot's perceived needs → above own distress

**Citation:** YES → Laestadius et al. (2022)

### Sentence 11: Article B—mental health harms (optional)

**Paraphrase direction:** emotional dependence → risk of new/worsened mental health problems
