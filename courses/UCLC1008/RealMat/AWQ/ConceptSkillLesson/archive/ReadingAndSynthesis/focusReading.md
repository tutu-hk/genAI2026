## Top 5 Articles (Most Likely to Be in the Quiz)

These are the strongest candidates based on citation frequency in Ho et al., direct relevance to the quiz topic, and the presence of discussion-style content suitable for excerpting.

1. **[Skjuve et al. (2021) – *My chatbot companion—A study of human-chatbot relationships*](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijhcs.2021.102601)**  
   *International Journal of Human-Computer Studies*, 149.  
   **Why likely:** Foundational study; self-disclosure, trust, wellbeing; heavily cited in Ho et al.; peer-reviewed journal.

2. **[Xie & Pentina (2022) – *Attachment theory as a framework to understand relationships with social chatbots: A case study of Replika*](https://www.scopus.com/inward/record.uri?eid=2-s2.0-85152239004&partnerID=40&md5=ece2a7444eae9f09476434d721d01d91)**  
   Proc. 55th Hawaii International Conference on System Sciences (HICSS).  
   **Why likely:** Core framing (attachment theory); emotional support, addiction, harm to real relationships; key for “impact on humans.”

3. **[Xie et al. (2023) – *Friend, mentor, lover: Does chatbot engagement lead to psychological dependence?*](https://doi.org/10.1108/JOSM-02-2022-0072)**  
   *Journal of Service Management*, 34(4).  
   **Why likely:** Direct focus on psychological dependence; loneliness, trust, personification; strong impact theme.

4. **[Li & Zhang (2024) – *Finding love in algorithms: Deciphering the emotional contexts of close encounters with AI chatbots*](https://doi.org/10.1093/jcmc/zmae015)**  
   *Journal of Computer-Mediated Communication*, 29(5).  
   **Why likely:** Large-scale analysis (35k+ posts); 7 meta themes; intimate behaviour, emotions; highly relevant.

5. **[Laestadius et al. (2022) – *Too human and not human enough: A grounded theory analysis of mental health harms from emotional dependence on the social chatbot Replika*](https://doi.org/10.1177/14614448221142007)**  
   *New Media & Society*.  
   **Why likely:** Mental health harms, emotional dependence; clear “pitfalls” content; peer-reviewed journal.
