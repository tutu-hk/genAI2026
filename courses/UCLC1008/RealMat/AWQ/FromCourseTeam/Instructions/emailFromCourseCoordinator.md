Please note the following important information regarding your first writing assignment in UCLC1008 - Academic Writing Quiz (AWQ):   

  

1. Date, Time and Duration of the Test 

  

The Test will be conducted in Week 6 (23-27 Feb 2026). Your class lecturer will inform you of the exact date in due course. 
The Test lasts for 50 minutes. There will be no break. 
You are not allowed to leave the classroom (e.g. going to the toilet) during the Test. 
  

2. Test Format 

  

You are required to summarise, paraphrase, and synthesise the main claims or arguments from TWO journal article excerpts on the topic "The impact of AI chatbots and companion apps on humans" in NO MORE THAN 300 words.
To prepare for the test, please read:
AWQ Instructions for Students (the cover page of the answer sheet)
a review article entitled "Potential and Pitfalls of Romantic Artificial Intelligence (AI) Companions: A Systematic Review" by Ho et al. (2025). The excerpts included in the test papers are drawn from articles listed in the reference section of this review article.
Both documents are available on Moodle [Individual Section > Academic Writing Quiz (15%)]
The test paper used in Semester 1 has been incorporated into the Module 2 handout (Part 5, pp. 50–60) for practice purposes. 
Remember to bring your own stationery. You can use a pen or pencil in your writing. 
This is a closed-book test. You are not allowed to consult the course handouts, your own material or resources (printed or electronic), or use any electronic devices (e.g. laptop computers, iPad, tablet PCs, mobile phones) during the Test. 
If you have special educational needs (SEN) arising from different types of disabilities and require reasonable adjustment and support for your studies, you need to register as an SEN student with the University. For details, please visit AR’s webpage. Please also immediately inform your class lecturer.  
 

3. Make-up Test   

  

Make-up tests will be allowed only upon presentation of a valid medical or official leave certificate within FIVE calendar days from the test date (e.g., if the test is scheduled for Monday, the supporting document should be submitted to your class lecturer on or before Saturday). 
To facilitate the make-up arrangement, please also provide your timetable and contact number when submitting the supporting document(s). 
  

If you have any questions, please contact your class lecturer.
