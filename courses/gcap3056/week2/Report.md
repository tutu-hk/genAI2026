# GCAP3056 Week 2 Report
## Course: Taking a Stand - Turning Research Insights into Policy Recommendations

---

## Overview
Week 2 served as an orientation session focusing on course objectives and methodologies. The lecture introduced students to critical policy analysis through a practical example of a government anti-smoking advertisement campaign, demonstrating how to identify policy failures and develop strategies for advocacy.

---

## Key Topics Covered

### 1. Course Platform & Administrative Setup
- **Learning Management System Introduction**: Students were introduced to the course's custom platform and required to register with a platform ID for course participation
- **WhatsApp Group Communication**: Integration of messaging platforms for class coordination
- **Data Privacy and Security**: Discussion of platform security limitations and the need for student verification

### 2. Critical Policy Analysis Framework
The instructor demonstrated a systematic approach to policy critique using a real-world example:

#### **Case Study: Anti-Smoking Advertisement at Bus Stops**
- **Problem Identification**: A government anti-smoking notice at bus stops featured overly complex Chinese and English text that created confusion rather than clarity
- **Opportunity Cost Analysis**: 
  - The large billboard space contained redundant legal language
  - Space could have been better utilized for public health education about secondhand smoking risks
  - Alternative uses could include government announcements or public service messages

#### **Design and Communication Issues**:
- Confusing message presentation that overwhelms rather than informs the public
- Excessive legal disclaimers taking priority over the core message
- Missed opportunity to explain the rationale behind policy (health implications of secondhand smoke)
- URL errors in the original advertisement (missing "www" prefix)

### 3. Policy Research and Advocacy Tools
Students were introduced to practical tools for government accountability:

#### **Government Telephone Directory**
- A comprehensive database of Hong Kong government departments and officials
- Key contacts for various government agencies
- Description: "The nuclear weapon" for policy advocacy

#### **Escalation Hierarchy**
- **Level 1**: Direct contact with relevant government department (e.g., Tobacco and Alcohol Control Office)
- **Level 2**: Department heads (e.g., Director of Health)
- **Level 3**: Senior government officials (e.g., Permanent Secretary)
- **Level 4**: Chief Executive's Office (escalation to chief decision-maker)

#### **Advocacy Methods**
- Email correspondence with responsible departments
- Letters to the editor for public awareness
- Complaint letters with specific recommendations
- Documentation (photographs) of policy failures
- Escalation to higher authorities when initial responses are inadequate

### 4. Strategic Communication Approach
- **Clear Problem Statement**: Define what is wrong and provide evidence
- **Solution-Focused Advocacy**: Present recommendations for improvement, not just criticism
- **Hierarchy Navigation**: Understand departmental structure to identify decision-makers
- **Professional Persistence**: Follow up through proper channels while maintaining respect for the process

---

## Course Objectives Reinforced

The course aims to teach students to:
1. **Critically Review Government Decisions** - from major policy changes to minor communication failures
2. **Conduct Evidence-Based Analysis** - use specific examples to support arguments
3. **Develop Recommendations** - provide actionable solutions, not just criticism
4. **Apply Advocacy Strategies** - use legitimate channels to pressure for change
5. **Understand Public Administration** - navigate government structures effectively

Aligned with SDG 3 (Good Health & Wellbeing) and SDG 4 (Quality Education).

## Actionable Insights for Students

### Immediate Actions
1. **Complete Platform Registration**: Obtain and share your platform ID as instructed
2. **Join Communication Channels**: Connect with classmates via WhatsApp group for peer collaboration

### Learning Skills to Develop
1. **Policy Document Analysis**: Learn to identify issues in government communications and policies
2. **Research Methods**: Master use of government databases and directories
3. **Writing Skills**: Draft effective complaint letters and recommendations
4. **Strategic Thinking**: Develop understanding of institutional hierarchies and decision-making processes

### Practical Application
- Identify one policy or government decision that you believe needs improvement
- Use the Government Telephone Directory to find the responsible department
- Draft a constructive suggestion that addresses both the problem and proposes solutions
- Understand that change requires persistence and proper channels

### Key Principles
- **Not Just Criticism**: Always pair problem identification with recommendations
- **Institutional Knowledge**: Understanding how government works is essential for effective advocacy
- **Evidence-Based Advocacy**: Use specific examples and data to support arguments
- **Professional Respect**: Maintain civil communication while pushing for change
- **Strategic Escalation**: Know when and how to escalate your concerns to higher authorities

## Conclusion

Week 2 established the intellectual and practical foundation for the course. Students learned that policy critique is not about theoretical criticism but about understanding institutional structures and using legitimate channels to drive real change. The emphasis on tools (Government Telephone Directory) and escalation hierarchies provides students with concrete strategies for advocacy that extend beyond the classroom.

The course position is clear: students have a role to play in improving government decisions, and with proper training and tools, even individual citizens can make a difference in public policy.
