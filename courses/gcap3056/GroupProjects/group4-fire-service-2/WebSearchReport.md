# Web search report — Group 4 Fire Service 2

**Project:** Group 4 — Fire Service 2 (fire service policies, operations, public safety).  
**Purpose:** To find public info on FSD operations, equipment, training, and prevention to support focus and hypotheses.  
**Method:** 5 rounds completed in one go; each round informed by the previous. All results include **clickable links**, **titles**, **dates**, **summaries**, **relevance to project goals**, and **follow-up suggestions**.

---

## How searches were conducted

- **Tool:** Web search (cursor/IDE integrated).
- **Rounds 1–5:** Round 1 (operations, equipment, training); Round 2 (year-end review, LegCo); Round 3 (recruitment, training); Round 4 (Fire Safety Ordinance); Round 5 (response time, performance pledge).

## Round 2 — Year-end review and LegCo

| Query | Domain | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Hong Kong Fire Services Department year end review annual report 2024 | info.gov.hk, news.gov.hk, hkfsd.gov.hk | FSD 2024 performance and initiatives |
| LegCo Hong Kong Fire Services Department funding equipment site:info.gov.hk 2024 | info.gov.hk | LegCo and funding context |

**Results (Round 2):**

1. **[Fire Services Department Year-end Review 2024 (with photo)](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202501/22/P2025012200234.htm)**  
   - **Date:** 22 Jan 2025 (covers 2024).  
   - **Summary:** 37,828 fire calls (+4.8%); 95.6% building fire calls within graded response time (target 92.5%); 14,701 people rescued; 33 fatalities, 444 injuries; 5 firefighter deaths. Major incidents: No. 3 alarm Jordan (Apr 10), No. 4 alarm Hung Shui Kiu construction site (Apr 9–11, ~42 hours, first use of indoor locating equipment). 460,400 inspections; 17,775 Fire Hazard Abatement Notices; Fire Safety (Buildings) Ordinance amendment in force Dec 13; Building Improvement Special Duty Team and One-stop Co-ordination Office planned.  
   - **Relevance to project goals:** Direct evidence on **operations** (response, incidents), **equipment** (indoor locating), **prevention** (inspections, ordinance). Use for hypotheses on adequacy of resources, response times, and old-building safety.  
   - **Follow-up:** Use for drafting questions to FSD/LegCo on equipment rollout, training for new tech, and old-building inspection capacity.

2. **[Fire Safety (Buildings) (Amendment) Bill 2024 to be gazetted on Friday](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202407/03/P2024070300421.htm)**  
   - **Date:** 3 Jul 2024.  
   - **Summary:** Amendment Bill gazetted; defaulted works mechanisms and increased penalties; Fire Safety Improvement Works Subsidy Scheme up to 60% of improvement costs.  
   - **Relevance:** Supports **prevention** and **building safety** focus.  
   - **Follow-up:** Trace passage of the Bill and implementation; ask FSD/BD about enforcement and subsidy take-up.

## Round 3 — Recruitment and training

| Query | Domain | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Hong Kong FSD recruitment training firefighter 2024 | hkfsd.gov.hk | Recruitment and training standards |

**Results (Round 3):**

1. **[Recruitment detail information \| Fire Services Department](https://www.hkfsd.gov.hk/eng/recruitment/recruit/index.html)**  
   - **Summary:** Official recruitment portal; links to Fireman/Firewoman (Operational/Marine), FSD Recruitment Connect, application process.  
   - **Relevance to project goals:** Directly addresses **training** and **personnel**.  
   - **Follow-up:** Compare entry requirements and 26-week foundation training with other jurisdictions; ask FSD about attrition and specialist training.

2. **[Fireman/Firewoman (Operational/Marine)](https://www.hkfsd.gov.hk/eng/recruitment/recruit/fireman.html)**  
   - **Summary:** HKDSEE Level 2 (five subjects including C&E); eyesight, physical fitness, job-related test; written aptitude test; fluent Cantonese; Basic Law and National Security Law Test; **26-week residential foundation training** at Fire and Ambulance Services Academy.  
   - **Relevance:** Evidence for **training** quality and duration.  
   - **Follow-up:** Ask whether training covers new equipment (e.g. indoor locating); compare with international standards.

## Round 4 — Fire Safety (Buildings) Ordinance

| Query | Domain | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Fire Safety Buildings Ordinance Hong Kong Cap 572 fire prevention | gov.hk, bd.gov.hk, info.gov.hk | Legal framework for fire prevention |

**Results (Round 4):**

1. **[Fire Safety (Buildings) Ordinance - Wikisource](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Fire_Safety_(Buildings)_Ordinance)**  
   - **Summary:** Cap 572 text; came into operation 1 Jul 2007; composite/domestic buildings (on or before 1 Mar 1987); fire service installations (sprinklers, hydrant/hose reel, alarm, emergency lighting, ventilation cut-off), means of escape, fire-resisting construction, means of access for firefighting.  
   - **Relevance:** Core **prevention** and **equipment** (building-side) framework.  
   - **Follow-up:** Cross-check with BD/FSD enforcement and Phase 1/2 implementation.

2. **[LCQ10: Fire safety measures of composite and domestic buildings](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/201707/05/P2017070500698.htm)**  
   - **Date:** 5 Jul 2017.  
   - **Summary:** Director of Buildings vs Director of Fire Services remit; fines for non-compliance (e.g. up to $200,000 and daily $20,000 for Compliance Orders).  
   - **Relevance:** Enforcement and penalties; useful for questions on effectiveness of enforcement.  
   - **Follow-up:** Ask for 2024 enforcement statistics and number of prosecutions.

## Round 5 — Response time and performance pledge

| Query | Domain | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Hong Kong Fire Services response time performance pledge 2024 | hkfsd.gov.hk, info.gov.hk | Official targets and 2024 achievement |

**Results (Round 5):**

1. **[Fire Services Department - Performance Pledge](https://www.hkfsd.gov.hk/eng/aboutus/performance.html)**  
   - **Summary:** Building fire calls — built-up 6 min (92.5% target), dispersed 9–23 min (94.5%); emergency ambulance 92.5% within target time; imminent fire hazard complaints 100% within 24 hours.  
   - **Relevance:** Direct **operations** and **effectiveness** metrics.  
   - **Follow-up:** Use with Year-end Review (95.6% achieved) to ask whether targets should be raised and how resources will support that.

2. **[Fire Services Department Year-end Review 2024 (with photo)](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202501/22/P2025012200234p.htm)**  
   - **Date:** 22 Jan 2025.  
   - **Summary:** 95.6% building fire calls within graded response time; 96.1% emergency ambulance within target; 100% imminent fire hazard complaints within 24 hours.  
   - **Relevance:** Confirms **exceeding** pledge; supports narrative that FSD meets current targets.  
   - **Follow-up:** Ask whether 95.6% is sufficient for high-risk areas; link to Jordan/Hung Shui Kiu incidents and old-building inspections.
